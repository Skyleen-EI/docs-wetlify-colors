# Image Picker

Go in the [Image Picker](https://colors.wetlify.com/image-picker) tool.

The Image Picker tool lets you extract dominant colors from an image or pick colors individually.

## Choose your image

First you need to choose your image.

You can recover it by URL by putting the URL of the desired image, then clicking on the **Replace** button.

But you can also recover it from your images by clicking on the **Select image** button.

## Extract colors

### Single color

When your image has been selected, you can directly pick a color from the image by clicking on the desired location.

You see at the bottom right of your cursor the color you are currently on.

You can also see this color on the far right of your color palette.

### Dominant colors

You can also generate the dominant colors of the palette by clicking on the **Generate** button.

You can also optimize the generation by adjusting the different parameters:

- **Color distance**: From 0 to 1 is the color distance to not have near colors (1 distance is between white and black)
- **Hue distance**: From 0 to 1
- **Saturation distance**: From 0 to 1
- **Lightness distance**: From 0 to 1

## Your palette

Your palette is located at the bottom right.

### Open your palette

Once your colors have been generated, you can open your palette by clicking on the **Open** button.

### Save your palette (requires an account)

You can save your palette by clicking on the **Save** button.

### Edit palette

You can modify your palette by switching to each color in it individually.

You can delete or save a color. To copy a color, simply click on the color's hex code.
